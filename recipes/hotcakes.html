Hotcakes

A hotcake (pancake or , griddlecake, or flapjack) is a flat cake, often thin and round, prepared from a starch-based batter that may contain eggs,

milk and butter and cooked on a hot surface such as a griddle or frying pan, often frying with oil or butter. It is a type of batter bread.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour.
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar.
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda.
  • 1 teaspoon salt.
  • 1 quart buttermilk.
  • 5 eggs.
  • 1 half cup of butter, melted.
  • 1 half cup of wheat germ.

Step by Step guide:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together all ingredients until thoroughly combined. Transfer to a large jar or container with lid.

  2. Heat a lightly buttered griddle over medium-high heat. Drop batter by large spoonfuls onto the griddle and cook until bubbles form and the edges are dry, 2 to 3 minutes. Flip and cook until browned on the other side, 2 to 3 minutes
    more. Repeat with remaining batter.
